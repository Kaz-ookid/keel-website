Privacy policy

Last updated: 19 July 2026

Who is responsible. Keel is made and run by one person: Maxime Teuber, based in Switzerland (the "I" below and, in legal terms, the data controller). Contact: contactkeel@protonmail.com.

Your data stays on your device. Commitments, accounts, payment history and settings live in the app's local storage and in the local backups you create. Keel has no server, no account system, no analytics and no advertising. I cannot see your data.

Network requests. Keel makes two kinds of request, and only these. Exchange rates are fetched from the public Frankfurter API (European Central Bank reference rates); the request is identical for every user and carries no personal data. Brand logos are off until you choose to turn them on: with the choice enabled, a commitment that has a brand website loads its logo through Google's public favicon service, and that request contains the brand's domain (netflix.com, for example) and, like any web request, your IP address. Google may process such requests on servers outside Switzerland and the EU, including in the United States. The switch in Settings turns logo loading off again at any time; logos already on your device keep showing without any further request. Items without a website never trigger a logo request. None of this reaches me: the requests go from your device to those services, and I store nothing about them.

AI import. With "Use your own AI", you share your list with the assistant you choose, under that provider's terms; Keel only reads the answer you paste back, on your phone. Where the on-device option is available, reading happens with Apple Intelligence on your iPhone and nothing leaves the device.

Reminders are scheduled locally by iOS. The app lock uses Face ID, Touch ID or your passcode through iOS; Keel only receives whether it succeeded.

Backups and exports are files you control. Sharing one sends it wherever you choose; treat exported files like the financial data they contain.

Retention and deletion. Because everything lives on your device, deletion is in your hands: remove entries in the app, or delete the app to erase all of its data (backup files you exported somewhere else are yours to delete there). There is no account to close and no server copy to request. If you email me, I keep the conversation only as long as it takes to help you, then delete it.

Your rights. Swiss data protection law (FADP) and, where it applies, the EU GDPR give you rights over your personal data: access, correction, deletion and objection, and the right to complain to a supervisory authority (in Switzerland the FDPIC, in the EU your national authority). In practice, the only personal data I ever hold is our email correspondence, so exercising these rights usually means asking me to delete a thread, which I will.

This website is static, sets no cookies and runs no analytics.

Changes. If this policy changes in a way that matters, the date above moves and the change is visible on this page.
